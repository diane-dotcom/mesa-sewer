What sewer repair involves

From camera inspection to a permanent fix

Every job starts with a sewer camera inspection. We run a scope down the line to see exactly what's wrong, root intrusion, a cracked or broken sewer pipe, a collapsed section, bellied pipe, or an old cast-iron line that's rusted through, and we show you the footage.

Boulder County is hard on sewer lines. Mature tree canopy means aggressive root intrusion into older clay and cast-iron laterals, and decades of freeze-thaw shift and crack pipe joints. Once we've diagnosed it, we recommend the lightest fix that actually solves the problem.

When the pipe path allows, we repair or replace it trenchless. CIPP pipe lining or pipe bursting renews the line through existing access points, with little to no digging. When a line is fully collapsed or off-grade, traditional excavation is the right call, and we'll tell you straight.

Trenchless vs. traditional dig

What's the difference?

Both methods fix a failed sewer line, but the disruption, timeline, and lifespan are worlds apart. Here's an honest breakdown.

Yard & driveway Trenchless (Recommended) Traditional Open-Trench Dig
Yard & driveway Minimal digging, landscaping stays intact Full trench across the yard, often the driveway too
Time on site Most jobs done in 1 day Several days of digging, repair, and backfill
Pipe lifespan Seamless liner rated 50+ years Depends on material & joints
Total cost Lower once restoration is factored in Pipe is cheaper; landscape repair adds up fast
Cleanup Little to no surface restoration Re-sod, re-pave, re-landscape after

Trenchless isn't right for every line, a fully collapsed or off-grade pipe still needs excavation. After the camera inspection we'll recommend the method that genuinely fits your line and your budget, not the most expensive one.
